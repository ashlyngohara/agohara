Welcome to my portfolio!

Hello! My name is Ashlyn O'Hara and I'm excited to welcome you to my digital portfolio. I am a political data reporter with five years of reporting experience, during which I covered two state legislative sessions and Missouri's 2018 Senate midterm election in addition to countless other hearings, debates and events.

In May I graduated summa cum laude from the University of Missouri School of Journalism with a Bachelor of Journalism in Data Journalism. Until May, I worked at The Columbia Missourian as an advanced statehouse reporter, which I did for two sessions in a row because I love it so much. I also worked on the graphics desk, where I helped reporters visualize their stories in addition to pitching and creating my own data visualizations using Adobe Illustrator, Tableau and Flourish.studio software.

I transferred to the University of Missouri in Fall of 2018 from the Eugene Lang College of Liberal Arts at The New School in New York City, where I studied Journalism + Design and Politics. Living in New York City was one of the greatest adventures of my life. I am originally from sunny San Diego, California, and have always had an obsession with big cities. As a child I treasured family trips to Los Angeles and San Francisco, and knew when I grew up I wanted to move somewhere big.

I've been working as a reporter since I was 16 and make the most of every experience. I've followed U.S. Senate candidates on the campaign trail, conducted interviews from the middle of the Missouri River and been embeded in the militant occupation of school spaces by students. There's no field better suited to accomodate my endless curiosity about absolutely everything in the world.

In my free time, you can find me on long drives in my Toyota Prius, binge-watching documentaries with my kitten Linda, or coding a new project (like this website!). I'm a big fan of folk music, an even bigger fan of veggie burgers and love using verb conjugations as a form of meditation.

Take a look around and reach out with questions, comments or book recommendations!
