Welcome to my portfolio!

Hi, I’m Ashlyn. Welcome to my digital portfolio! I am a government and education reporter at The Peninsula Clarion — a 52-year-old daily newspaper serving Alaska’s Kenai Peninsula.The Clarion is the only comprehensive source of information for the Kenai Peninsula, which is about the size of West Virginia and is home to more than 58,000 people.

I graduated in 2020 summa cum laude from the University of Missouri School of Journalism with a bachelor’s degree in data journalism. While at Mizzou I spend two years reporting for The Columbia Missourian, including as an advanced statehouse reporter covering the Missouri Legislature and on the paper’s graphics and visuals team.

I hail from Chula Vista, California, and spent time in New York City and Missouri before landing in Alaska in the middle of the COVID-19 pandemic. In the more than two years I’ve spent at the Peninsula Clarion in Kenai, I’ve been able to better understand what work is important to me in this field.

As one of three full-time reporters at the Clarion I am responsible for covering three city councils, the Kenai Peninsula Borough Assembly, the Kenai Peninsula Borough Board of Education and our state legislative and congressional delegations. I believe the best kind of journalism is that which demystifies the public processs and presents policy issues as human-centered stories.

In my freetime, you can find me wandering around used bookstores, taking daytrips around southcentral Alaska or spending time with my cat, Linda.

Take a look around and reach out with questions, comments or book recommendations!
